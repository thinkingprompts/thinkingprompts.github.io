Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

Physiological Needs

  • Food
  • Water
  • Air
  • Shelter
  • Clothes
  • Sleep

Safety Needs

  • Health and wellness
  • Personal security against accidents and injury
  • Emotional security
  • Financial security

Love and Social Belonging Needs

  • Family
  • Frienship
  • Intimacy
  • Trust
  • Acceptance
  • Receiving and giving love and affection
  • Social groups
  • Community groups
  • Churches and religious organizations

Esteem Needs

  • Self-Respect:strength, competence, mastery, self-confidence, independence, and freedom
  • Respect from Others: status, recognition, fame, prestige, and attention

Cognitvie Needs

  • Creativity
  • Foresight
  • Curiosity
  • Meaning

Aesthetic Needs

  • Beautify one's life
  • Appreciation of beauty around one's self

Self-Actualization Needs

  • Realization of one's fully potential
  • Partner acquisition
  • Parenting
  • Utilizing and developing talents and abilities
  • Pursuing goals

Transcendence Needs

  • Giving oneself to somthing beyond oneself, for example, altruism or spirituality
